## 🚀 1148. Article Views I

### 💡 Idea

The goal is to find the authors who **viewed at least one of their own articles**.

A user views their own article when the `author_id` is the same as the `viewer_id`. We simply filter such records, remove duplicates, and return the author IDs in ascending order.

---

### 💻 Solution

```sql
SELECT DISTINCT author_id AS id
FROM Views
WHERE author_id = viewer_id
ORDER BY id;
```

---

### 🛠️ How It Works

1. Select records where `author_id` is equal to `viewer_id`.
2. Use `DISTINCT` to remove duplicate author IDs since an author may view multiple articles.
3. Rename the `author_id` column as `id`.
4. Sort the result in ascending order of `id`.

---

### ⏱️ Complexity

**Time Complexity:** `O(n)` *(single scan of the table)*

**Space Complexity:** `O(n)` *(stores distinct author IDs)*
